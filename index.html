Medley

- the smart search for your movies

After a long day of designing and coding we love to just collapse on the couch and watch some Netflix. But it is often difficult to find good movies.

So we set out to solve this problem.

Medley combines data from Netflix, IMDb, and Rotten Tomatoes. We wrote the first iteration over a 36-hour hackathon. Since then we've refined our product and have released a beta version with limited search capabilities.

What I did

  • general concept and branding
  • visual and interaction design using Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop
  • front-end development using HTML, CSS, JS

Reka Szepesvari Photography

From the first moment I laid my hands on my DSLR camera I have fallen in love with photography. There is something special about capturing precious moments.

I built this website for two reasons: I wanted to escape the limitation of templates when showcasing my photos and I wanted to learn how to build a jQuery carousel from scratch.

I looked at tons of jQuery slideshows and noted elements I liked and disliked. In the end, I came up with a new design that I felt would work well for my purpose.

What I did

  • developed minimalist design concept using a pencil and paper approach
  • developed website using HTML/CSS and JS
  • learned and utilized jQuery
  • implemented complex functions such as "infinite scroll"

Twine

Students at the University of Waterloo have created groups on Facebook to buy and sell textbooks, offer and look for rides, and sublet apartments. However, it is nearly impossible to find relevant information on Facebook.

In the spring of 2014 I had the idea to create a website to help students find books, rides, or places to live more easily.

What I did

  • created a list of functionalities
  • loosely sketched the layouts for the pages using pencil and paper
  • played around with various colour palettes
  • created "mock-ups" for various pages using Adobe Photoshop

I began designing the layout and visual look of the website, but unfortunately this projects has been put on the back burner.



Conference on Learning Theory
